Senior High School Tracks

Be college-ready with the knowledge, training, and preparation to pursue a degree, start a business, or gain employment.

Academic Track

The Academic Track is designed for students who want to pursue higher education. It offers four strands: Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM), Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS), Accountancy, Business and Management (ABM), and General Academic Strand (GAS).

STEM

Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics

STEM is an approach to learning and development that integrates the areas of science, technology, engineering and mathematics.

HUMSS

Humanities and Social Sciences

The Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) strand covers topics in the liberal arts, training students to think, write, and speak about various humanistic and societal concerns.

ABM

Accountancy, Business and Management

The ABM strand focuses on the basic concepts of financial management, business management, corporate operations, and all things that are accounted for in running a business.

GAS

General Academic Strand

The GAS strand is designed for students who are undecided on which track to take. It offers a mix of subjects from different strands.

Technical-Vocational-Livelihood Track

A curriculum that is a combination of Core Courses and specialized hands-on courses that meets the competency-based assessment of TESDA.

ICT

INFORMATION & COMMUNICATIONS TECHNOLOGY

Under ICT curriculum, students will learn how to write programs and design websites. This will also help them hone their skills in animation and illustration which will help them thrive in the creative field.

HE

Home Economics

Track that teaches students job-ready skills and knowledge for livelihood projects. The HE strand is part of the Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL).

IA

Industrial Arts

The IA strand equips students with technical skills in carpentry, electronics, and other industrial fields, preparing them for hands-on careers.
